Why two stages

Detecting whether a frame is usable and identifying which anatomy it shows are different judgments. Separating them keeps each model's features focused, and makes failures diagnosable — a wrong answer is either "missed a real plane" or "confused two real planes," never both at once.

What the model gets wrong

Trans-ventricular and trans-thalamic brain planes are adjacent slices of a continuous sweep. The model confuses them roughly 29% of the time on held-out data — and Grad-CAM shows it's looking in the right neighborhood when it does, not at noise. The ambiguity is partly in the anatomy itself.

Limitations

Trained on the FETAL_PLANES_DB dataset (~12,400 images, ~1,792 patients). Not validated for clinical use. Stage 1 catches non-standard frames about 87% of the time — false passes are possible and Stage 2's output should be read with that in mind.
